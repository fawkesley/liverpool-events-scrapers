Connecting Cities

Working in partnership with 14 cultural organisations from 11 European cities, Connecting Cities aims to create a network of public screens and ‘urban media’ to distribute artistic and social content throughout the whole of Europe.

Friday 6 September - Saturday 7 September

  • Details

Across Europe, there are a huge number of public screens, facades and urban media but they are all almost always used for purely commercial purposes. They are also generally controlled locally and operated completely separately. Connecting Cities wants to open up access to these screens and use them to present new artistic and social content whilst creating a cultural exchange locally and internationally.

The Connecting Cities project aims to use the communicative and networking potential of urban media facades, to contribute to and strengthen the European identity. Community and identity building isn't created through the consumption of advertisement - it is not created through the thoughts of a single person or a company's interests, but through the collective debates and actions of citizens from across Europe.

Connecting Cities will set up and test this European infrastructure over four years through a series of events, workshops, conferences and artists' programmes like research residencies and mobile urban media labs.

The Connecting Cities Network was initiated by Public Art Lab and further developed and realised together with the following co-organisers: Ars Electronica GmbH Linz, iMAL Brussels, m-cult Helsinki, Medialab-Prado Madrid, Media Architecture Institute Vienna, Riga 2014, Videospread Marseille, BIS Body Process Arts Association Istanbul, The Museum of Contemporary Art Zagreb. MUTEK Montreal, Marseille Provence 2013 and Aarhus University accompany the project as associated partners. Additional partners will enrich the project throughout the years with their expertise.

To keep up to date with the project, sign up to the Connecting Cities newsletter at www.connectingcities.net

With the support of the Culture Programme of the European Union

Current Projects at FACT

TransEurope Slow
Until 15 September

Take a virtual bike ride and explore the development of European cities through the eyes of the urban cyclist. In April 2013, Uncoded Collective visited Liverpool to meet with local community groups in order to digitally render a new, narrative picture of the city. This innovative approach combines the playfulness of computer games with an open design methodology to explore the ways in which we discover our home cities, and those further afield.

Ready to Cloud
6 - 7 September

With specially modified fog-machines, interactive technologies and projectors, The Constitute have created a unique new tool to allow you to communicate through a cloud. The cloud is connected with Bauhaus in Dessau, Germany. Scanned visitors in both spots will be digitally teleported into the cloud where they can meet each other. 

Binoculars To... Binoculars From...
From 14 August

Look through a set of extraordinary binoculars created by artists Varvara Guljajeva and Mar Canet Sola and you'll see a real-time window onto another city. But peeping toms beware, the inhabitants of the city can see a huge image of your eye looking over them
