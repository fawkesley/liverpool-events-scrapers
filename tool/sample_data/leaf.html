    Kin Creative Network Presents: Thinking Session2 - Liverpool-Location or Global Production Hot spot?

    08/08/2013

    The Liverpool Film Office (LFO) is the longest established independent resource of its kind in the UK. Over the past 10 years the LFO has attracted an estimated £100m of inward investment into the local economy and has been responsible for attracting large scale Hollywood feature films to the city. But whilst Liverpool boasts that it is “the most filmed city in the UK outside of London” this is only part of the economic picture. The city is home to a number of independent film companies who are creating very successful productions despite a difficult financial environment and limited studio and post production facilities.

    MC'd by Neil Atkinson of The Anfield Wrap and Film First, this session invites the audience to discuss the challenges faced by the local independent film sector, how individual companies are overcoming these challenges and how the independent community, the city and industry bodies could work together put Liverpool on the global production map. Panellists: Roy Boulter - Hurricane Films Cllr.Paul Brant - Liverpool City Council (Deputy Mayor) Caroline Cooper-Charles - Creative England Neil Fitzmaurice - North Star Productions Dave Hughes - Red Union Films Sol Papadopoulos - Hurricane Films

    5.30pm - 6.30pm, followed by a Kin social from 6.30pm - 8.30pm / First Floor / Free entry but registration required: http://www.eventbrite.com/event/7590925663

    Silent Sleep

    01/08/2013

    Silent Sleep return from their jaunt to Australia playing shows with Dr. Karl Kennedy (FROM BLOODY NEIGHBOURS!!) and head straight to LEAF to play songs from their debut album, Walk Me To The Sea, which has been receiving rave reviews from the likes of The Guardian, Louder Than War and airplay from BBC 6Music. The project of one Chris McIntosh, Silent Sleep bring warm, heartfelt melancholic pop and will be performing with full band on the night. Also on the bill are long-time Liverpool favourites Voo, whose blend of beautiful melodies and intricate guitar work recall bands like Husker Dü and Dinosaur Jr. The line up is completed by Puzzle, whose nostalgiac guitar-pop, led by brother and sister David and Lucy Johnson, makes for a stellar line-up.

    Doors 8pm / First Floor / £2
